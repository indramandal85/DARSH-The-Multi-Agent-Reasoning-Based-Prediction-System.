RBI Emergency Rate Hike Shocks Markets: May 2022

The Reserve Bank of India made a surprise off-cycle rate hike of 40 basis points on May 4, 2022, raising the benchmark repo rate to 4.4 percent. The decision was made at an unscheduled monetary policy committee meeting, shocking financial markets that had expected the RBI to wait for its June policy meeting.

Governor Shaktikanta Das announced the decision citing rising inflationary pressures, with retail inflation already above the RBI's upper tolerance band of 6 percent. The Russia-Ukraine war had pushed global commodity prices sharply higher, with crude oil above 100 dollars per barrel threatening to fuel domestic inflation further.

The Sensex crashed over 1400 points intraday following the announcement, its biggest single-day fall in months. The Nifty50 fell 1.5 percent as bond yields spiked sharply. Banking stocks and interest-rate sensitive sectors like real estate saw the heaviest selling.

Finance Minister Nirmala Sitharaman said the government supported the RBI's inflation-fighting measures and that the priority was price stability. The Ministry of Finance noted that the global inflation environment required decisive central bank action.

Economists were divided on the move. Some praised the RBI for acting decisively before inflation became entrenched. Others warned that hiking rates while global supply shocks were the primary cause of inflation could hurt growth without solving the underlying problem.

NASSCOM expressed concern that higher borrowing costs would reduce access to capital for technology startups. Industry body CII called for the RBI to balance inflation control with supporting the ongoing economic recovery.
