India Posts Record 20.1 Percent GDP Growth: August 2021

India's economy grew at a record 20.1 percent year-on-year in the first quarter of financial year 2021-22, the Central Statistics Office announced on August 31, 2021. The surge was driven by a low base effect from the same period last year when the economy contracted sharply during the first COVID-19 lockdown.

The strong growth numbers significantly beat analyst expectations and signaled that India's economic recovery was gaining momentum. Manufacturing output rose 49 percent while construction activity surged as infrastructure spending accelerated. The services sector also showed strong recovery led by finance, real estate and professional services.

Finance Minister Nirmala Sitharaman welcomed the data saying it confirmed that the government's economic recovery strategy was working. She highlighted that capital expenditure on infrastructure had been a key driver and announced additional spending would be maintained.

The Reserve Bank of India maintained its accommodative monetary policy stance, keeping interest rates at historic lows to support the recovery. Governor Shaktikanta Das said the recovery was becoming more broad-based and the central bank would continue to support growth while monitoring inflation.

Stock markets reacted positively with the Sensex and Nifty touching record highs in the following weeks. Foreign institutional investors increased their allocation to Indian equities as the strong growth data improved the outlook for corporate earnings.

Business confidence surveys showed the highest readings since before the pandemic. NASSCOM reported that the technology sector had emerged stronger from the crisis and was hiring aggressively to meet global demand. Consumer spending was recovering as vaccination rates increased and COVID restrictions eased.
